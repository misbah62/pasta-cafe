L'ARTE DELLA SEMPLICITÀ

The hands that shape tradition.

At Settantatré, we believe the finest things are born from patience. From the heritage grains of Umbria to the golden yolks of pasture-raised eggs, our story is written in the ingredients.

The Artisan's Touch

Every strand of pasta that leaves our kitchen carries the warmth of the hands that formed it. Our pastaio, Alessandro, learned the craft in his grandmother's kitchen in Trastevere, where the only tools were a rolling pin and a deep respect for the season.

We don't use industrial extruders. We use bronze dies and human intuition, ensuring each texture is perfectly calibrated to hold the sauce that will eventually grace it.

The Provenance

Purity in every grain.

Heritage Grains

We source exclusively from three organic farms in the Val d'Orcia. These ancient varieties of wheat offer a depth of flavor and nutritional profile that modern commercial grains simply cannot match.

Pasture-Raised Eggs

The vibrant hue of our pasta comes from eggs laid by hens that roam free on the Roman hills. Rich in Omega-3s and golden in color, they provide the silky structural integrity our dough demands.

IL PROCESSO

Slow & Steady

"Great things are not done by impulse, but by a series of small things brought together."

Most commercial pasta is flash-dried at high temperatures in minutes. Ours is slow-dried for 72 hours in temperature-controlled rooms that mimic the natural breezes of the Italian coast. This preserves the organoleptic properties of the grain and creates a porous surface that catches sauce like a masterpiece.
